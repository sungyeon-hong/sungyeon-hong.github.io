Musical Homeostasis v.2: Distributed Tuning

A triangular lattice of musical agents. Each cell has a preferred note, listens to nearby cells, and adjusts its current note according to local harmonic compatibility. The whole grid becomes a collective instrument: what you hear is the aggregate chord produced by the field.

What this models

This demo embodies self-organisation as distributed tuning. No cell sees the whole grid, and there is no conductor. Each cell only responds to its local musical neighbourhood.

Over time, an initially disordered field may settle into unison, chordal regions, oscillation, persistent tension, or a new equilibrium after disturbance.

Rules of the agents

  1. Each triangle has a preferred note, shown as a small dot.
  2. Its main colour shows the current note it contributes to the collective sound.
  3. At every step, it listens to neighbouring triangles and evaluates possible notes.
  4. It balances local harmony, memory of its own preference, inertia, and noise.

Interaction guide

  • Press Initialise, then Run.
  • Listen for whether the sound settles, drifts, or remains turbulent.
  • Use Disturb patch to test recovery and resilience.
  • Ask: does equilibrium mean sameness, or compatible difference?

Pitch distribution

The distribution determines the sound: a dominant pitch yields near-unison; several compatible pitches yield a chord; scattered pitches create tension.

Questions to explore

  • When does the system converge, and when does it keep moving?
  • Does disturbance return the field to the same state or a new one?
  • Which balance of listening, self-memory, and noise feels most alive?
  • Can compatible difference be more interesting than perfect agreement?
Homeostasis here is not stillness. It is ongoing regulation: the field adapts locally while trying to preserve enough coherence to remain recognisably musical.
